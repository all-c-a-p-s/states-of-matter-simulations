Melting

Melting occurs when the temperature of a solid increases to the melting point. The melting temperature of a material is dependant on a range of physical and chemical factors and is defined as the energy required to overcome the intermolecular forces holding the particles in a the rigid structure (or lattice). During melting, particles in the solid gain energy and vibrate more vigorously until they begin to break the bonds holding them in a lattice, thus forming a liquid.
