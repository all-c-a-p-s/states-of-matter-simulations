Solid

In a solid, particles are tightly packed and vibrate about a fixed point. This means they cannot move from their position in the structure, but an increase in temperature will cause them to vibrate more vigorously (this can be explained as temperature is defined by the average kinetic energy of particles in a given region). If the temperature of the solid reaches absolute zero (0°K/-273°c/-460°f) the particles will be completely static in their fixed points.
